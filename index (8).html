What is a UPI QR Code?

A UPI QR Code is a machine-readable barcode that encodes your Unified Payments Interface (UPI) payment details. When someone scans this code using a UPI-enabled mobile app, they're instantly directed to a payment screen pre-filled with your UPI ID, payee name, and optionally, a fixed amount and description.

Introduced by the National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI), UPI QR codes have revolutionized how payments are made at retail stores, restaurants, online platforms, and between individuals. With over 10 billion UPI transactions processed monthly in India as of 2026, QR code payments have become the dominant payment method for millions of merchants and consumers.

How UPI QR Codes Work

A UPI QR code encodes a UPI deep link in the following format:

upi://pay?pa=yourname@bank&pn=Your+Name&am=100.00&tn=Payment+Note&cu=INR

When a user opens their UPI app and scans the QR code, the app reads this URL and opens the payment confirmation screen. The payer reviews the details and authorizes the payment using their UPI PIN.

Key Parameters in a UPI QR Code

  • pa – Payee UPI ID (e.g., yourname@okaxis)
  • pn – Payee Name (displayed to the payer)
  • am – Amount (leave blank to let the payer enter any amount)
  • tn – Transaction Note or description
  • cu – Currency code (INR for Indian Rupee)

Use Cases & Benefits of UPI QR Codes

UPI QR codes are incredibly versatile and can be used across a wide range of scenarios:

  • Small Business & Retail: Print your QR code on a standee or display at your shop counter. Customers pay without needing change or a card machine.
  • Freelancers & Professionals: Add your QR code to invoices, business cards, or email signatures. Clients can pay you with a single scan.
  • Restaurants & Food Stalls: Include the QR on menus or table cards for contactless bill payments.
  • E-commerce & Websites: Embed the QR code on your website's checkout page for direct UPI payments.
  • Events & Donations: Use QR codes for event entry fees or collecting donations at fundraisers.
  • Peer-to-Peer Payments: Share your QR code with friends and family for easy money requests.

Tips for Using Your UPI QR Code

Here are some best practices to get the most out of your UPI QR code:

  • Always test before printing: Scan your QR code once before distributing it to ensure it works correctly.
  • Use SVG for printing: Download the SVG version for large-format printing (banners, flex boards) to maintain sharpness.
  • Leave amount blank for flexibility: Don't set a fixed amount if you want customers to enter their own amount.
  • Keep your UPI ID active: Ensure your UPI ID is linked to an active bank account before sharing the QR code.
  • Add a payee name: Always include your name or business name so payers can verify they're paying the right person.
